African Elefant

An elephant's trunk is an amazing thing. The trunk has over 40,000 muscles and is used to smell, grab food and other objects, lift, drink, dig, and balance. Finger-like appendages at the tip of the trunk are able to pick up very small objects like twigs.

  • Height: 3.3 m
  • Weight: 6000 kg
  • Life span: 70 years old
  • Diet: Herbivore

They are the largest land mammal alive today. The genus consists of two extant species: the African bush elephant, L. africana, and the smaller African forest elephant, L. cyclotis. African elephants are found widely in Sub-Saharan Africa, in dense forests, mopane and miombo woodlands, Sahelian scrub, or deserts. These animals are herbivores and eat over three hundred pounds of food daily.
